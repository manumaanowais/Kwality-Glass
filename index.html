STRUCTURAL GLAZING

Facade Glazing

Facade glazing refers to the practice of using glass and other transparent or translucent materials to create the exterior skin or envelope of a building. This technique has gained significant popularity in modern architecture due to its aesthetic appeal, energy efficiency, and the potential for creating visually striking and innovative building designs. Facade glazing can be found in a wide range of building types, including commercial, residential, institutional, and even industrial structures.

Canopy Glazing

Canopy glazing refers to the practice of using glass or other transparent materials to create a covered structure that extends from the exterior of a building. Canopies are typically designed to provide shelter from the elements while allowing light to penetrate the space beneath. They serve both functional and aesthetic purposes and can be found in various architectural contexts, such as entrances, walkways, outdoor seating areas, and more.

Skylight Glazing

Skylight glazing refers to the practice of using glass or other transparent materials to create windows or openings in a building's roof, allowing natural light to enter interior spaces. Skylights are designed to bring daylight into areas that might not have direct access to windows, such as corridors, stairwells, or interior rooms. They offer numerous benefits, including enhancing the aesthetics of a space, reducing the need for artificial lighting, and providing a connection to the outdoor environment.
